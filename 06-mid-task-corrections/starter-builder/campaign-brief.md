# Q3 Product Launch Campaign Brief — Relay

## Product
Relay is a team messaging app for distributed teams that replaces Slack. Core differentiator: every channel has a built-in async mode where messages queue until the recipient's working hours, eliminating notification fatigue across time zones.

## Launch Goals
- 2,000 beta signups in first two weeks
- 40% activation rate (send first async message within 24 hours)
- 15 earned media mentions (target: remote work publications)
- Email sequence: 5-part onboarding drip, 60% open rate on email #1

## Target Audience
**Primary:** Engineering managers at companies with 50–200 employees across 3+ time zones
**Secondary:** Remote-first startup founders (seed to Series A)

## Messaging Pillars
1. "Your team is not slow. Your tools are synchronous."
2. "Async by default. Real-time when it matters."
3. "Time zones are a feature, not a bug."

## Competitive Landscape
- Slack: dominant, but async is bolted on (scheduled send, later). No native timezone awareness.
- Teams: enterprise lock-in. Async story is nonexistent.
- Twist (by Doist): closest competitor, but no working-hours queue. Thread-first, not channel-first.
- Discord: consumer-first. No business features.

## Brand Voice
- Confident but never condescending
- Technical enough for engineers, clear enough for founders
- Short sentences. Active voice. No jargon unless the audience uses it daily.
- Never say: "revolutionize," "game-changer," "disrupt," "synergy"

## Budget
$12,000 total
- $4,000 paid social (LinkedIn + Twitter/X)
- $3,000 content creation (blog posts, case study)
- $2,000 influencer seeding (5 remote-work thought leaders)
- $1,500 email tooling (Beehiiv + Loops)
- $1,500 launch event (virtual demo day)

## Timeline
- Week 1–2: Content creation + influencer outreach
- Week 3: Soft launch to waitlist
- Week 4: Public launch + paid campaign
- Week 5–6: Optimization + retargeting

## Open Questions
- Should the onboarding email sequence lead with the pain point (notification fatigue) or the solution (async queuing)?
- Do we position against Slack directly or avoid naming competitors?
- Is "async messaging" the right category term, or should we coin something more specific?

## Assets Needed
- Landing page copy (hero, features, social proof, CTA)
- 5-email onboarding sequence
- 3 blog posts (launch announcement, founder story, use case walkthrough)
- Social media kit (10 posts across LinkedIn/X)
- Press kit (one-pager, founder bio, product screenshots)

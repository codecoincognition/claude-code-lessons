# Launch Campaign Brief — Nova Analytics v2

## The product
Nova Analytics v2 — a customer analytics dashboard for SaaS founders. We're launching **Multi-Channel Alerts**: a feature that pings you on Slack, email, or SMS the moment a customer hits a churn-risk signal (dropped NPS, usage cliff, billing failure, support escalation).

## The audience
~18,000 founders and ops leaders on our newsletter. 72% are 2–20 person SaaS companies. They've seen two competitors ship "alerts" features this quarter (one heavy, one lightweight). They're alert-fatigued and skeptical of yet another notification product.

## What's different about ours
- **Risk signals, not vanity metrics.** We alert on churn risk, not "user logged in."
- **One signal, many channels.** Set it once, route it smart. Quiet on weekends if you want.
- **Retrospective tuning.** Every alert comes with "should this have fired?" feedback that retrains the threshold.

## Voice & tone
- Friendly, not corporate. We talk to founders like a peer, not a vendor.
- Concrete over abstract. Numbers, scenarios, and specific customer moments beat adjectives.
- Never use: "game-changer," "revolutionize," "unleash," "unlock the power of."
- We do use: "we built this because…", "the boring truth is…", "here's what broke for us."

## Launch constraints
- Email goes out **Tuesday 10am ET**.
- Must include: what the feature does, one concrete example, and a clear CTA ("Turn it on" → in-app link).
- Must NOT include: pricing changes (we're not changing pricing), comparison to specific competitors by name, or "limited time" pressure framing.
- Length target: under 200 words body + subject line.

## The positioning tension (this is the hard part)
Three directions, all defensible:

1. **Feature-first** — lead with "Multi-Channel Alerts is live." Clean, direct, tells people what's new.
2. **Outcome-first** — lead with "Stop finding out about churn on Friday at 5pm." Emotional hook, slower to explain.
3. **Contrarian** — lead with "We almost didn't build this. Here's why we changed our mind." Voice-forward, risks burying the news.

We don't know which is right. The team is split.

## What we know about the audience's inbox this week
- Our biggest competitor shipped their version 9 days ago with a feature-first email. It performed okay (12% open rate).
- Founders are tired of "introducing…" emails. Last month's newsletter (which opened with a story) hit 34% open rate — our best of the year.
- Our Tuesday 10am slot competes with two other SaaS newsletters our audience also reads.
